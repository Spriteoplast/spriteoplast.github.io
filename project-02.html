Park on the Gowanus canal in Brooklyn, NY. The design of this park takes inspiration from the salt water marshes that used to make up the Gowanus neighborhood, before the area got industrialized for shipping. This park could nurture plant and animal life in Gowanus after the canal is cleaned up. Plant vessels of various sizes and shapes can hold a range of wetland vegetation, and water streams flow through the park, theoretically eroding the land masses. Formal elements derived from rule based drawings, and erosion patterns in marshlands. The park accounts for a variety of water levels, making the park resilient in the case of above-average water levels.
